Bunker Information

Built in 1900 to store the coal for the nearby Tiumpan Head Lighthouse, The Bunker’s foundations pay homage to its hardy past, accompanied with aluminum cladding with reflective capabilities that help blend The Bunker into the landscape.

Through the entrance, you’ll find a westward facing bedroom hosting a super king-sized bed. A spacious, luxurious bathroom with a walk-in rainfall shower, designed impeccably with natural materials, textures and colours. The utility room is also situated downstairs, with space for muddy boots, as well as a washer and dryer.

A spiral staircase leads up to an open plan kitchen and living space with windows encompassing the room, sliding glass doors and a reinforced glass balcony to accommodate the sweeping views. The kitchen comes fully equipped with everything you need for a self-catered stay, including a Nespresso coffee machine for your morning coffee. A wood burner centres the living space which offers cosy sofas, snuggly rugs, as well as a tv and a telescope for stargazing.

Guest access:
The entire property is available for guests including private outdoor space with parking, patio, balcony, slipway into the sea and beach access.

Where you will be

Points of interest in the area

The bunker is located on the shore right next to the small fishing village of Portnaguran. The village is at the tip of the Eye Peninsula, locally known as the Point. Tuimpan Head Lighthouse is a short walk away. The viewpoint is one of the best whale-watching spots in Europe. An abundance of wildlife can be spotted in the bay, including dolphins, porpoises, sea otters, seals, whales, and numerous seabird species.

Getting around the area

There is ample private parking on the property. A car is necessary to explore the Lewis and Harris islands. There is a regular local bus service that passes right in front of the bunker in the village if you wish to go to Stornoway without taking your car.
